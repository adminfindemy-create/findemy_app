# 2. The Problem

Findemy is a two-sided product, so there are two problems — one for learners, one for academies. They're the same gap seen from opposite ends: **there is no organised, trustworthy place where skill learners and skill academies find each other.**

## 2.1 For students / learners

Today, finding the right academy is **time-consuming, manual, and opaque.** Someone who wants to learn guitar typically:

- Searches Google for "guitar classes near me" / "best dance academy in [area]"
- Wades through a long list of generic results, map pins, and outdated listings
- Opens multiple academy websites or Instagram pages, most of which are thin
- **Calls academies one by one** to ask the same questions
- Asks about fees, batch timings, and trial availability — with no standard answer
- Tries to **compare** options that present their info completely differently
- Hunts for reviews scattered across Google, JustDial, and social media
- Has no reliable way to verify quality or whether seats are even available

The result: high effort, low confidence, and many people simply give up or settle for whatever is closest.

**Core student pains**

| Pain | What it feels like |
|------|--------------------|
| No dedicated place to look | Skill academies aren't indexed anywhere coherent |
| Hard to compare | Every academy presents fees/timings differently |
| No transparency | Fees and schedules are unclear until you call |
| Too many manual inquiries | Phone calls and DMs just to get basics |
| Can't judge quality | Reviews are scattered or absent |
| No low-risk way to try | Trials exist but are hard to discover and book |

## 2.2 For academies

Most skill academies — especially independent gurus and small studios — are **great at teaching and weak at being found.** They:

- Have limited digital visibility; they rank below aggregators and ads
- Depend heavily on **word-of-mouth** and walk-ins
- Struggle to reach interested learners nearby
- Have weak or no online branding (a patchy Instagram at best)
- Get outshone by larger institutes with marketing budgets
- Have no simple tooling to handle inbound interest, trials, and attendance

**Core academy pains**

| Pain | What it costs them |
|------|--------------------|
| Low discoverability | Empty seats despite real local demand |
| Word-of-mouth dependence | Unpredictable, slow growth |
| Weak online presence | Lost trust vs. polished competitors |
| No lead/trial management | Inbound interest leaks away |

## 2.3 Why now

- Skill learning (music, dance, art, fitness) is a large, durable, and growing offline market in India.
- Smartphone and UPI penetration make app-based discovery and payments normal behaviour.
- The supply side (academies) is **highly fragmented and unorganised** — exactly the condition where an aggregator that also gives them tools can win.
- No category-defining product owns "skill academy discovery" the way food, travel, or beauty have their platforms.
