# 9. Market Analysis

> **Status:** Research compiled 2026-06-17. Sources span 2021–2026.
> **Method note:** Findings below were produced by a fan-out deep-research pass (24 sources fetched, 105 claims extracted, 25 put through 3-vote adversarial verification). Only claims that survived verification are stated as fact. A separate **"Refuted / do not cite"** section lists widely-repeated numbers that *failed* verification — these are recorded so the team doesn't accidentally reuse them in a deck.

---

## 9.0 The headline you need to internalise first

**There is no credible, verifiable top-down market size for Findemy's actual core market** — offline, all-ages skill / hobby / performing-arts academies in India. We looked hard. Every neat "$X billion extracurricular market / Y million searches" figure circulating online came from a single secondary blog source and **failed adversarial verification** (see §9.7). The honest position is:

- The *adjacent* offline-fitness market **is** credibly sized (Deloitte/HFA) and is a useful proxy for "fragmented offline supply that an aggregator can organise."
- The *core* market must be sized **bottom-up by the team** (count academies × batches × fees in Delhi-NCR), not lifted from a report. This is an opportunity as much as a gap: it means no incumbent has authoritatively claimed or measured this category yet.

Treat any single big TAM number in a pitch as a liability unless you built it yourself from defensible unit economics.

## 9.1 Market size

### What is credibly established (adjacent proxy)
India's **offline fitness-facility market** — the closest well-measured analogue to Findemy's supply side — was approximately:

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Market size (2024) | ~₹16,200 cr | Deloitte × Health & Fitness Association (HFA) India Fitness Market Report 2025 |
| Centres | 46,500+ | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |
| Members | ~12.3 million | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |
| Penetration | ~0.8% of population | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |
| Projected size (2030) | ~₹37,700 cr | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |
| CAGR | ~15% | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |
| Fastest-growing segment | Boutique studios, ~19% CAGR | Deloitte/HFA 2025 |

**Why this matters for Findemy:** the fitness market is *highly fragmented with very few national operators* — exactly the structural condition (lots of small, hard-to-find independent supply) where a discovery + booking aggregator creates value. Boutique studios growing fastest mirrors Findemy's target supply (independent gurus, small studios). Sub-1% penetration signals a category still early in formalisation.

**Caveat:** this is an *adjacent proxy, not Findemy's TAM.* It covers gyms/fitness only, not music/dance/art/yoga academies, and no Delhi-NCR-specific slice was reliably sized.

### What you must build yourselves (core TAM/SAM/SOM)
No verified figure exists for the offline all-ages skill/hobby/performing-arts academy market or its Delhi-NCR slice. **Recommended bottom-up approach:**

- **SOM (Delhi-NCR, near-term):** count addressable academies in NCR (music + dance + art + yoga + fitness studios) → estimate avg batches/academy → avg students/batch → avg fee → apply realistic trial→enrol conversion → apply your commission rate. This gives a defensible serviceable revenue number you actually control.
- **SAM:** extend the same unit model to top metros (Phase 1 expansion markets).
- **TAM:** national rollout of the same model.

This bottom-up number will be far more credible in front of investors than any borrowed top-down figure — *and* doubles as your operating model.

## 9.2 Competitive landscape

No competitor was found that occupies Findemy's exact position — **all-ages, offline, trial-first, two-sided, skill-academy discovery + booking in Delhi-NCR.** Competitors are either under-capitalised, narrow (kids-only), or online-only.

### Direct / nearest-adjacent

| Player | Model | Scope | Funding / scale (verified) | Gap Findemy can exploit |
|--------|-------|-------|----------------------------|--------------------------|
| **UrbanPro** | Tutor/training-institute marketplace | National, tutor-centric, inquiry/lead model | Modestly capitalised: **~$2.47M total raised**, ~$7M valuation (2017), ~$1.65M FY24 revenue *(medium confidence; rests on one secondary source)* | Lead-gen, not trial-first booking; tutor-centric not academy-centric; thin on offline academy experience |
| **BeeBuddy** | Discovery + booking | **Delhi-NCR** (Delhi, Noida, Gurugram, Faridabad, Ghaziabad) | Private | **Kids-only (2–12)** — covers the *same categories* (piano, kathak, ballet, zumba, painting, pottery, yoga) but the **entire adult segment is open** |
| **CurioBoat** | Activity centres + discovery | Delhi-NCR, four centres | Private | **Kids 3–14**, geographically tiny; not a broad open marketplace |
| **Yellow Class** | Live online hobby classes for kids | Online, pan-India | **$6M Series A led by Elevation Capital (Aug 2021)**, after $1.3M seed (Nov 2020) | **Online + kids only** — the offline, trial-first, in-person wedge is wide open |
| **Sulekha** | Local services directory | National | Established | **No standardised pricing** — Delhi dance listings require you to call each academy. Directly validates Findemy's comparison/transparency value prop |

### Adjacent / substitutes (what people use today)
- **Google / Maps search** — generic, unstructured, no fees/timings/trials/comparison/booking.
- **JustDial / Sulekha** — directory + lead-gen spam; thin, unverified info; **no standardised pricing** (verified for Sulekha); no trial/enrol flow.
- **Instagram / word-of-mouth** — social proof but doesn't scale discovery, no comparison, no structured booking.
- **cult.fit / fitness aggregators** — own fitness, but vertical-specific and largely first-party/owned-inventory, not an open academy marketplace.

**Read on the field:** the competitive set is *shallow on Findemy's exact axis.* The two NCR players that look closest (BeeBuddy, CurioBoat) are **kids-only**; the funded player (Yellow Class) is **online-only kids**; the broad incumbents (UrbanPro, Sulekha) are **lead-gen directories without trial-first booking.** That is a genuine white space — but it also means the category is unproven, so the burden is on Findemy to demonstrate the trial-first loop works.

## 9.3 Trends & tailwinds (verified)

- **UPI / digital payments are mainstream.** UPI is **~83% of digital payment volume** (end-2024), **16–17B transactions/month**, **~390M users**. App-based discovery-to-payment is normal consumer behaviour — Findemy's pay-for-trial flow rides an established rail.
- **Coaching demand skews urban.** Private-coaching participation is **30.7% urban vs 25.5% rural**, with urban households spending **~₹3,988 vs ~₹1,793 per student/year** (MoSPI CMS Education Survey 2025). *Caveat: this measures academic coaching, not hobby classes* — but the urban > rural spend pattern is the relevant signal for an NCR-first launch.
- **Fragmented offline supply, formalising slowly.** The fitness proxy (sub-1% penetration, 46,500+ mostly-independent centres, boutique growing fastest) shows a supply side ripe for aggregation.

> Note: several "creator economy / post-COVID resurgence / X-million-searches" tailwind claims that show up in blogs were **refuted** (§9.7). The verified tailwinds above are narrower but solid.

## 9.4 Target segments & demand signals

- **Demand is real but unquantified from the top down.** The strongest *verified* demand signal is structural, not a headline number: urban households already pay meaningfully for out-of-school learning, payment rails are ubiquitous, and existing discovery options (Sulekha, Google) are demonstrably poor (no standardised pricing, no booking).
- **The clearest under-served wedge is adults.** Every NCR-specific competitor found is **kids-only.** Findemy's all-ages positioning means the **adult passion-learner and serious/aspiring-learner segments are effectively uncontested** by a dedicated discovery+booking product in NCR.
- **The parent-for-child segment is contested** by BeeBuddy and CurioBoat in NCR — expect to compete on supply density, trial UX, and trust here rather than walk into open space.

**Actionable:** consider leading go-to-market with the adult segments (less competition, faster to seed credible supply, clearer trial→enrol intent) while treating kids as a fast-follow where you'll meet incumbents.

## 9.5 Business-model benchmarks (take-rate)

- **Indian local-services take-rates top out around 25–30%.** Urban Company charged **up to 30%** on beauty before worker protests forced a cut to **25%** (Oct 2021). That's the practical *ceiling* and a cautionary tale: **high commissions trigger supplier backlash.**
- **Implication for Findemy's commission model:** academies are price-sensitive small businesses, and your supply is your cold-start bottleneck. A take-rate anywhere near 25–30% on enrolments would invite exactly the supplier-revolt / disintermediation dynamics that sank goodwill for others. The trial commission (small, per-trial, framed as "pay only when you get a qualified lead") is the defensible wedge; enrolment commission should be modest and clearly justified by ongoing value (management tooling, repeat demand), not a one-time toll.

> The team's open business-model questions (commission %, who bears gateway fees, free-trial treatment, refunds) in `06-business-model.md` remain the right ones. Benchmark says: **anchor low, prove value, and avoid the 25–30% zone for recurring enrolment revenue.**

## 9.6 Risks & marketplace dynamics

- **Cold start is the central risk** (already correctly identified in `07-differentiation.md`). The single-city, supply-first focus is the right textbook response — concentrate density before breadth.
- **Disintermediation / platform leakage is structurally severe for a trial-first model.** This is worth flagging sharply: a learner and academy meet **in person after a single booking.** Nothing physically stops them transacting off-platform for the recurring enrolment — which is exactly where the lifetime value sits. Standard marketplace-leakage mitigations apply (keep payments/scheduling/management on-platform, make the academy admin app genuinely indispensable for attendance/batch/enrolment ops, bundle ongoing value like reviews and future community). **The enrolment commission is the most leak-prone revenue line; the management tooling is your anti-leak moat.**
- **Supply quality / curation** matters early — discovery is only as good as the academies on it.
- **Barriers to entry are low for a basic clone but high for a dense, trusted, two-sided NCR network** — your moat is supply density + verified-trial reviews + the admin app's operational lock-in, not the app UI.

## 9.8 Strategic implications for Findemy

1. **Own the category definition.** No one has credibly sized or claimed "offline skill-academy discovery." Build the bottom-up TAM yourself and you simultaneously get a defensible investor number *and* the operating model. Don't borrow a refuted top-down figure.
2. **Lead with adults in NCR.** It's the segment no local competitor touches; kids is contested by BeeBuddy/CurioBoat.
3. **Comparison + transparency is a proven, demonstrable wedge.** Sulekha literally shows no standardised pricing — your normalised, side-by-side compare is a concrete, verifiable improvement over what learners use today.
4. **Trial commission is the safe revenue wedge; enrolment commission is the leak risk.** Keep enrolment take-rate well below the 25–30% local-services ceiling, and make the academy admin app indispensable so the recurring relationship stays on-platform.
5. **The fitness proxy is your best external validation slide.** A credible Deloitte-sized, fast-growing, fragmented adjacent market shows the structural thesis (organise fragmented offline supply) works — use it as directional support, clearly labelled as adjacent.
6. **Open questions that genuinely need primary research** (none had a verifiable public answer): the real size of the NCR offline academy market; academy willingness-to-pay on trial vs enrolment commission; disintermediation rate after first trial; adult-focused competitive density in NCR. These are field-research / pilot questions, not desk-research ones.
