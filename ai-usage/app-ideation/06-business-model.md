# 6. Business Model

## 6.1 How Findemy makes money (now)

**Commission on transactions** — Findemy takes a fee when a learner **books a trial** or **enrols** in a batch through the platform. This is the only monetisation in the MVP.

Why this model first:
- **Incentive-aligned.** Findemy earns only when it delivers an academy a real, paying outcome (a trial-taker or an enrolled student). Academies aren't asked to pay upfront just to be listed.
- **Low friction for supply.** "Pay only when you get a student" is the easiest pitch to acquire academies in a new city — critical for solving the cold-start problem.
- **Rails already exist.** The apps already handle payments and apply a platform fee, so the mechanism is in place.

## 6.2 Future revenue streams (post-MVP)

As the marketplace gains density and trust, additional streams open up (see [`08-roadmap.md`](08-roadmap.md)):

- **Academy listing fees** — recurring fee for academies to be listed / access richer tools.
- **Premium / featured listings** — academies pay for boosted visibility: top placement, featured badges, sponsored search/discovery slots.
- **Student passes** — a membership for learners that bundles **discounts** across academies (and potentially perks), creating recurring consumer revenue and stickiness.

These are intentionally **not** in the MVP — they make sense once there's enough supply, demand, and engagement for them to be worth paying for.

## 6.3 Open questions to settle (team)

These are deliberately unresolved and should be decided by the team — flagged here so they don't get lost:

- **Commission rate(s)** — what % on trials vs. enrolments? Flat vs. tiered?
- **Who bears payment-gateway fees** — Findemy, the academy, or the learner?
- **Trial pricing** — are trials always paid, sometimes free (academy's choice), or promotional? How does commission apply when a trial is free?
- **Refunds / cancellations** — commission treatment on cancelled or refunded bookings.
- **Payout terms to academies** — cadence and method.

> This document states the *model*; the *numbers* (rates, fees, terms) are TBD and owned by the team.
