# 7. Differentiation

## 7.1 What people use today

| Alternative | What it does | Where it falls short for skill academies |
|-------------|--------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Google / Maps search** | Finds nearby places, reviews, pins | Generic, unstructured; no fees/timings/trials; no transparency; no booking |
| **JustDial / local directories** | Lists businesses, phone numbers | Lead-gen spam; thin, unverified info; no trial/enrol flow |
| **UrbanPro / tutor marketplaces** | Connects to tutors/classes | Tutor-centric, inquiry/lead model; not academy-discovery + trial-first booking |
| **Instagram / word-of-mouth** | Social proof, referrals | Doesn't scale discovery; no transparency; no structured booking |
| **Academy's own website / WhatsApp** | Direct contact | Exists only if you already found them; inconsistent; manual |

The common thread: today's options help you *stumble onto* an academy, then dump you into **phone calls and manual back-and-forth.** None own the full **discover → try → enrol** journey for skill academies.

## 7.2 Why Findemy is different

1. **Category focus.** Purpose-built for skill academies (music, dance, art, yoga, fitness…), not a generic directory. The whole experience is shaped around how people actually choose a place to learn.
2. **Trial-first.** The product is built around booking a low-risk trial, the natural decision point — not just showing a phone number.
3. **Transparency.** Standardised, upfront academy info — fees, timings, modes, reviews — on every profile, so learners can evaluate and decide with confidence instead of calling around.
4. **Genuinely two-sided.** A companion **academy app** to manage profiles, trials, attendance, and enrolments — Findemy isn't just a listing layer, it's where academies run their intake.
5. **Community upside (later).** The roadmap (reels, recognition, events) adds engagement and a reason to keep coming back that pure directories can't match.

## 7.3 The moat (over time)

- **Dense local supply** in a focused market (Delhi-NCR first) that competitors can't quickly replicate.
- **Trust** from real reviews tied to real, verified trials and enrolments.
- **The trial → enrol loop** generating proprietary data on what learners want and which academies convert.
- Eventually, **community and brand** — being the place where skills are discovered *and* celebrated.

## 7.4 Honest risks (to keep in view)

- **Cold start** — must seed enough academies and learners in Delhi-NCR for the experience to feel full.
- **Disintermediation** — once introduced, a learner and academy could transact off-platform; the product must keep delivering enough value (convenience, trials, management tools, future community) to stay in the loop.
- **Supply quality** — discovery is only as good as the academies on it; curation and reviews matter early.
